About Me

Here is what I know. I’m often thought of as a quiet, reserved, and thoughtful individual. I don’t seek out special attention or social engagements.The idea of being alone is thrilling,
not taxing. These periods of solitude are crucial to my health and happiness. Whether I’m simply spending time resting or engaging in an activity, solitude is a welcome relief. I often enjoy reading, coding, baking, gardening, crafting, writing, gaming, watching movies, or
doing any other activity that’s performed alone.

I prefer a small circle of friends and that’s not a sign that I can’t make friends or don’t like
to socialize. In fact, I enjoy talking with people and getting to know others. I also prefer the solitude of a small circle of friends.
High-quality relationships are a key to my happiness.You may also say I’m introspective and curious. I find myself daydreaming or working things out in my mind long before  I put a plan of action in place or lift a single finger to change anything.

I have a very active inner thought process. That also leads me toward self-reflection and research. I am dedicated to pursuing my interests and feeling prepared and well-read. I’m accused of zoning out a lot. I often “escape” from a situation by zoning out or letting my
mind wander away from the task at hand. For me, this may be a way to leave a situation
that feels too chaotic or uncomfortable; it’s a survival mechanism of sorts. But to others, it may seem like I’m unfocused. I’m more comfortable writing out my thoughts rather than speaking, especially when I’m unprepared.

I prefer to think through my response because my communication style is focused and considerate. I can carry on conversations, but if decisions are necessary, I may want more time to consider and weigh my options so I feel confident in the choice.
